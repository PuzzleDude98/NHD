Creating Eliza

Eliza was an early chatbot. It was created at MIT in 1964-1965. It was named ELIZA after Eliza Doolittle, from the musical My Fair Lady.
Image of Eliza Doolittle [TODO]
At the time, Joseph Weizenbaum was a visiting proffessor at MIT, and ELIZA was his side project. ELIZA was mad in the MAD-SLIP programming languege, and it was an expiriment with human interaction. It was meant to simulate a Rogerian Phsycotherapist.

When people interacted with ELIZA, they often got into deep conversations with it. They felt like ELIZA knew what they said and was a real phsycotherapist. However, this was just an illusion. ELIZA was simply programmed to restate whaetver words you said in a different manner, and it did so with several key-response pair. When the key was detected in a users input, it would give a response that corresponded to that key.
