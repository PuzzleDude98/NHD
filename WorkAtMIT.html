Work At MIT

Joseph Weizenbaum was the major person involved in the build up to ELIZA, along with his students at MIT.
Joseph Weizenbaum had to create the MAD-Slip language in order to program ELIZA, and he got hired at MIT in 1964,
causing him to work on its infrastructure. People were interested in this because of its potential.
ELIZA was one of the first turing-complete machines, and it opened the floodgates for an AI revolution and the proliferation of chatbots.
ELIZA also caused people to use chatbots more, instead of customer support agents, resulting in what we have today.
